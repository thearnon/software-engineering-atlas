## How it appears in enterprise systems

RBAC, or Role-Based Access Control, assigns users to roles and lets each role define which actions are allowed, denied, or limited.

In enterprise internal web applications, RBAC usually appears around approval workflows, dashboards, reports, audit logs, and admin screens where access needs to be explicit and reviewable.

<Callout type="enterprise" title="Enterprise context">
  Inside internal systems, RBAC almost always travels with an approval workflow
  and an audit log. Designing the three together from the start avoids costly
  rework later.
</Callout>

## Things to watch

- Do not start with too many roles.
- Do not hard-code permission logic only in the UI.
- Keep audit logs for important actions.
- Add scope when departments, branches, or ownership boundaries matter.
