## How it appears in enterprise systems

An audit log records important system events: who created a request, who approved it, who changed access, and when it happened.

In enterprise internal tools, audit logs support traceability when teams investigate mistakes, disputes, or security reviews.

## How it connects to RBAC

RBAC says who is allowed to perform an action. An audit log records who actually performed that action and in what context.

Without audit logs, a system may know who should have access but not what happened after access was granted.

## Things to watch

- Do not log only successful actions; important denied actions can matter too.
- Do not store more sensitive data than the review process needs.
- Keep timestamp, actor, action, target, and enough context to investigate later.
- Make logs searchable for support, compliance, and incident review workflows.
